The Kitchen: Built to Actually Be Used

Two full islands anchor the kitchen — one for prep, one for gathering. It's finished with custom-built cabinetry, natural stone countertops, and a full KitchenAid appliance package, all under a designer lighting plan and a cathedral ceiling that opens the whole space up to the covered outdoor living beyond it.

Cathedral ceiling kitchen · Oversized hidden pantry

The pantry is one of those details most people don't think to ask about until they're living without one — oversized, hidden off the kitchen, with butcher block and custom shelving built in.

Living Spaces & Primary Suite

The living room runs wide, anchored by a fireplace wall with built-ins on either side and sliding doors that pull the outdoor living space into the sightline. Hardwood flooring runs through the main living areas, with custom trim and millwork carried throughout and high ceilings in every major room.

Living room · Primary bath with freestanding tub

The primary suite sits on a split bedroom plan, away from the secondary bedrooms, with a bath finished in tile to the ceiling, a freestanding tub, and a glass shower — and the utility room connects directly into the primary closet, which is a small detail that changes how a household actually runs day to day.
